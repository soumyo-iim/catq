Arithmetic Test 9- Ratio & Proportion (LOD 2)

Question 1

In 2016, two employees at a company, Alex and Jordan, had their salaries in the proportion 2:3. Over the course of a year, Alex's salary changed following a ratio of 3:4, while Jordan's salary adjusted at a ratio of 5:7. By 2017, the gap between their earnings amounted to Rs 2,300. What is the annual income of Jordan in 2017?

Options:

  • 1. Rs 6000
  • 2. Rs 6300
  • 3. Rs 6600
  • 4. Rs 6900

Question 2

A collector has a variety of coins in a collection, with coins of three different values. The proportion of coins worth Rs 1 to those worth Rs2 is 5:7 respectively, and the proportion of coins worth Rs 2 to those worth Rs 5 is 7:6 respectively. If the total monetary value of the Rs 1 coins in the collection amounts to Rs 15, determine the total value of the coins worth Rs 5.

Options:

  • 1. Rs 50
  • 2. Rs 70
  • 3. Rs 90
  • 4. Rs 110

Question 3

Total expenses of a hostel are partly constant and partly vary directly as the number of boarders. When the number of boarders are 120 and 100 the total expenses are Rs. 2000 and Rs. 1700 respectively. Find the total number of boarders if the total expense is Rs. 1880

Options:

  • 1. 100
  • 2. 112
  • 3. 115
  • 4. 118

Question 4

Sachin presents a special emerald necklace to Shruti on their anniversary. Unfortunately, the necklace breaks and splits into three segments, with their weights proportioned as 2:3:4. The worth of each segment is directly related to the square of its weight. Initially, the necklace was valued at Rs. 24300. Determine the  loss  in value caused by the damage.

Options:

  • 1. Rs 13600
  • 2. Rs. 14600
  • 3. Rs. 15600
  • 4. Rs. 16600
