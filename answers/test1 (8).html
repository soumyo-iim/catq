Arithmetic Test 8- Ratio & Proportion (LOD 2)

Question 1

The employee-manager ratio at a certain company was 25:4, but when the company laid off eighty employees for budgetary reasons, seventeen managers resigned in protest. Which of the following could be the new employee-manager ratio at the company?

Options:

  • 1. 63:12
  • 2. 95:17
  • 3. 33:5
  • 4. 55:6

Question 2

A toy store has only dolls, cars, and blocks in the ratio 3:7:10. The number of cars is more than the number of dolls by a number that is a multiple of both 5 and 8. What is the minimum number of toys in the store?

Options:

  • 1. 400
  • 2. 100
  • 3. 200
  • 4. 300

Question 3

A particular electronics store sells only laptops and tablets. The ratio of laptops to tablets is 3:4, the ratio of touchscreen tablets to non-touchscreen tablets is 7:3, and the ratio of premium laptops to regular laptops is 3:2. If there are 108 premium laptops, what is the ratio of non-touchscreen tablets to regular laptops?

Options:

  • 1. 1:1
  • 2. 1:2
  • 3. 1:3
  • 4. 2:3

Question 4

Anna wants to distribute chocolates among her four children in the ratio  1/3 : 1/4: 1/5 : 1/6. How many minimum chocolates should she buy, so that she can distribute the chocolates in the given ratio?

Options:

  • 1. 30
  • 2. 45
  • 3. 57
  • 4. 90

Question 5

On Sunday, in a pet shelter, the ratio of the number of cats to the number of dogs was 4:5. On Monday, the shelter received 35 more cats and dogs, then the ratio of cats to dogs became 5:8. Assuming that no animal left the shelter or was adopted and the shelter had cats and dogs only, what is the least number of dogs that were there in the shelter on Sunday?

Options:

  • 1. 55
  • 2. 60
  • 3. 65
  • 4. 70
