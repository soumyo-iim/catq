Arithmetic Test 1- Ratio & Proportion (LOD 1)

Question 1

The total marks scored by a student in three subjects are 339. If the ratio of marks scored in the first subject to those in the second subject is 3 : 7, and the ratio of marks scored in the second subject to those in the third subject is 5 : 9, then how many marks did the student score in the second subject?

Options:

  • 1. 95
  • 2. 100
  • 3. 105
  • 4. 110

Question 2

Four friends, Alex, Ben, Charlie, and David, are sharing a prize in a competition. The prize money is to be distributed among them in the ratio of 5 : 2 : 4 : 3. If Alex receives Rs 1,000 more than David, what is Charlie's share? 

Options:

  • 1. Rs 1000
  • 2. Rs 1500
  • 3. Rs 2000
  • 4. Rs 2500

Question 3

In a city, there are two types of vehicles, cars, and motorcycles. The ratio of the number of cars to the number of motorcycles is 5 : 3, with a total of 640 vehicles. If 30 more motorcycles are brought into the city, how many more cars should be introduced so that the ratio of cars to motorcycles becomes 14 : 9

Options:

  • 1. 15
  • 2. 20
  • 3. 25
  • 4. 30

Question 4

In a recipe for a cake, the ratio of sugar to flour is 5:7. If the recipe calls for 20 grams of sugar, then how many grams of flour are needed?

Options:

  • 1. 21
  • 2. 28
  • 3. 35
  • 4. 42

Question 5

In a classroom, the ratio of red pens to blue pens is 7:6. If 4 more red pens are added to the classroom and 3 blue pens are removed, then the ratio of red pens to blue pens becomes 4:3. What is the total number of red and blue pens initially in the classroom?

Options:

  • 1. 91
  • 2. 104
  • 3. 107
  • 4. None of these
